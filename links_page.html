Links

Here you will find links to websites, organizations, ideas, and other information about how we can create an alternative food landscape!

WWOOF – “Worldwide Opportunities on Organic Farms”

www.wwoof.org

“Worldwide Opportunities on Organic Farms” is a collection of national organizations that help promote work-trade opportunities on organic farms

Local Harvest.org

www.localharvest.org

Local Harvest is a site that aims to connect people all around the United States with locally produced, fresh and organic food. The structure of the site is community-based and enables users to share blog postings, events, information, and products

Bullock’s Permaculture Portal

www.permacultureportal.com

From their site: “The Portal serves as a resource for permaculture information as well as the online home for our extraordinary permaculture site on Orcas Island, the Bullock's Permaculture Homestead. For over 25 years we have applied our shared experience to create what experts refer to as the finest permaculture site in North America. Together we develop and implement practical solutions for sustainable living while offering hands-on permaculture courses, workshops, skill-building, and more.”

There you will find a wide variety of resources relating to permaculture, homesteading, and sustainable living

Farmer’s Market.com

www.farmersmarket.com

This website aims to help people find Farmer’s Markets with the admirable goal of making “local food as commonplace in the American kitchen as frozen pizza by making it easy to find and buy.”

Grow Food.org

www.growfood.org

Grow Food is a website similar in thought and design to wwoof-ing, aiming to connect workers and people interested in learning about organic farming and sustainable agriculture
